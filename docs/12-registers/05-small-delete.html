The Small Delete Register

"- — for charwise deletes that wouldn't be worth a numbered slot.

id: registers.small-delete · part: registers · keys: "- · lessons: 123

When you make a small charwise delete (less than a line, no newline), the deleted text goes into "-, leaving the numbered registers untouched.

x, dw on a tiny word, r{c} replacing one character — none of these should clobber your last big delete in "1. So Vim diverts them to "-, the small delete register.

🔧 Under the Hood — What Counts as Small

A delete goes to "- (and not into the numbered register chain) when it is:

  • Charwise (not linewise or blockwise).
  • Less than one full line.
  • Doesn't contain a newline.

Anything else — dd, dip, d% on a multi-line bracket — goes into "1 and shifts the numbered registers.

The unnamed register "" is still updated by both small and big deletes. So you can always paste the most recent delete with p, regardless of which numbered slot it landed in.

The practical upshot: you can x a typo or diw a small word freely without losing the line you carefully deleted into "1 for re-pasting.

Tip. The r{c} replace command writes the replaced character into "-. Useful occasionally for swap-back-what-I-just-replaced.
🔧 Under the Hood — "- is the small-cut side channel

Deletes that don't span at least one line and don't specify a register skip the numbered queue entirely and land in "-. The point is to keep the numbered queue full of meaningful chunks; tiny deletes (x, dl) would otherwise flush useful history out of "1–"9 immediately.
