Buffers

Files in memory, navigated with :ls and :b.

Keys: `:ls`, `:b`, `:bn`, `:bp`, `:bd`

Vim loads each file into a buffer. :ls lists all buffers. :b switches to one by name or number. :bn / :bp step through them. :bd unloads a buffer.

Every file you open in Vim becomes a buffer — an in-memory copy. You may have ten buffers loaded but only one window showing one of them. Navigating buffers, not windows, is how you move between files.

Tip. Ctrl-^ swaps to the alternate buffer (the one you were just in). It's the buffer-level version of ``. Most people use this dozens of times a day.
