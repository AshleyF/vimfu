Half- and Full-Page Scrolling

Ctrl-D / Ctrl-U / Ctrl-F / Ctrl-B — scroll without losing place.

id: scroll.half-full-page · part: scroll · keys: Ctrl-D, Ctrl-U, Ctrl-F, Ctrl-B · lessons: 86, 87, 88, 89

Ctrl-D scrolls down half a page; Ctrl-U scrolls up half. Ctrl-F is forward a full page; Ctrl-B is back a page.

Two ways to fly through a file: half-page and full-page scrolls. Half-page is gentler — your cursor and the surrounding context move together. Full-page is faster and good for jumping past large regions you don't care about.

🔧 Under the Hood — These Move the Cursor and the Viewport

Vim doesn't have a separate scroll position from the cursor. The viewport's top line is just getline(1) … (top + winheight - 1). When you scroll, both the visible top line and the cursor move. The two stay in step so the cursor never goes off-screen.

The distance for Ctrl-D/Ctrl-U comes from the 'scroll' option (default: half the window height). You can change it with :set scroll=10 or with a count: 10Ctrl-D sets 'scroll' to 10 and scrolls 10. So the count is sticky for next time.

Ctrl-F/Ctrl-B are full page. They're slightly less smooth than the half-page ones because the cursor and visible region jump farther in one keystroke.

Tip. If your terminal eats Ctrl-F (some shells use it for forward-search history), try the alternative bindings or remap. Ctrl-D/Ctrl-U are nearly always free.

Reference

Key Action
Ctrl-D Down half a screen (and update 'scroll')
Ctrl-U Up half a screen
Ctrl-F Forward a full screen
Ctrl-B Back a full screen
