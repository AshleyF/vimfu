More Ways into Insert

Five doors into insert mode — pick the one that lands the cursor where you want.

Keys: i, a, I, A, o, O

i and a are the two basic doors into insert mode. I, A, o, O are the convenience doors that combine a motion with the entry.

i inserts before the cursor. a appends after. That covers most of life — but Vim gives you four more doors that do a useful little motion on the way in.

Tip. Notice the pattern: the lowercase letter does the obvious thing at the cursor; the uppercase letter does the bigger version (start/end of line, or line above/below).
Tip. All six are changes. . repeats whatever you typed in insert mode (along with the entry command). So o-type-a-line, then ., opens another line below and types the same line again.
