The Change List

g; / g, — walk through the places you've been editing.

id: marks.changelist · part: marks · keys: g;, g,, :changes

Like the jump list, but for changes: g; jumps back to your previous edit position; g, walks forward.

Every change you make sets a position in a per-buffer list. g; walks back through it; g, walks forward. Different from the jump list — changes only, this buffer only.

🔧 Under the Hood — Change List vs Jump List

Jump list — per-window. Records positions you've been, regardless of whether you edited there. Used for navigating with Ctrl-O/Ctrl-I.

Change list — per-buffer. Records positions where you actually changed text. Used for revisiting your edits with g;/g,.

The special mark './`. is just "the most-recent entry of this buffer's change list." g;g;g; walks deeper into history, two-then-three-then-four edits ago.

Especially useful when you've been editing in many places in one file and want to revisit each spot to verify.

Tip. Combined with Ctrl-O/Ctrl-I, you have two complementary trails: places you've been and places you've changed. Different muscle memory; both invaluable.
🔧 Under the Hood — Change list is parallel to undo, not the same

Every modification stamps the cursor position into a separate change list. g; and g, navigate it without touching the buffer state. That separation is why 'go to where I last edited' works after you've undone, redone, or jumped away — the list records positions of edits, not snapshots of edits.
